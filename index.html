Overview

The DC Center is a joint initiative between Accenture and PeopleShores, designed to develop underrepresented talent for roles in the tech industry. Through an 12-month program, the center combines 4 months of intensive technical training with 8 months of real-world consultancy experience. In partnership with Per Scholas, a nationally recognized training organization, the DC Center leverages their instructors and curriculum, which has been vetted and signed off by Accenture to align with industry standards and project needs.

Program Structure

The program is structured in two distinct phases.

1. Technical Training (4 Months)

Apprentices complete a curriculum developed by Per Scholas and adapted with Accenture’s input, ensuring alignment with the skills and competencies required for their future roles.

2. Consultancy Phase (8 Months)

Following their training, apprentices transition to consulting roles on Accenture teams. This phase focuses on applying learned skills in real-world scenarios with the goal of securing full-time offers by the end of the program.

Curriculum Overview

The DC Center’s curriculum, delivered by Per Scholas instructors, has been carefully vetted and approved by Accenture to meet both technical and professional development needs. Key elements include:

Technical Training

  • CySA+: Threat detection, incident response, log analysis.
  • Splunk: Data monitoring, dashboards, alerts.
  • Data Analytics: SQL for visualization.
  • Emerging Tech: AI tools for software development.

Professional Development

  • Agile and Scrum Methodologies.
  • TEffective communication and teamwork.
  • Leadership and time management.

Day-to-Day Programming

Apprentices at the DC Center are paid employees from the start of the program. Their standard working hours are 8:30 AM to 5:30 PM, mirroring a professional environment to prepare them for their consulting roles.

  • Instructor-led technical sessions or hands-on coding exercises.
  • Project-based learning, including pair programming and peer reviews.
  • Focused self-study for skill development.
  • Completing labs and assignments to reinforce technical concepts.
  • Lead check-ins to address challenges and track progress.

Assessments

  • Knowledge-Based Assessments (KBA): Closed-book, close-ended multiple-choice quizzes administered at the end of each section to evaluate theoretical understanding.
  • Skill-Based Assessments (SBA): Project-based tasks conducted after most sections to assess the application of technical skills in practical, real-world scenarios.
